Our Aim!

We have created a project together named as "SMSL" (Study More, Stress Less). It is understandable how difficult it is to manage time, cope with exam stress and fear of failure since we are also students. Unfortunately, all these difficulties can have a detrimental effect on mental health and social life of students. This project aims to help students in increasing their motivation, introducing them to effective study methods and aiding them to manage their time and stress levels. We are having  some different webinars by education specialists who helps students with their valuable advice and methods.

Research about "academic stress"

The research by "National Institutes of Health", is aimed to investigate the amount of academic stress and associated risk factors among Saudi Arabian students who enrolled in HSPP (Health Science Preparatory Program)-adapted local institutions. After some surveys and research, the study found that "HSPP" students have an academic stress level of 8.37 (SD = 4.641) (according to SAAS scores), which can be considered to be high for a particular student. 

Reference: 
Alsulami, S., Al Omar, Z., Binnwejim, M. S., Alhamdan, F., Aldrees, A., Al-Bawardi, A., Alsohim, M., & Alhabeeb, M. (2018). Perception of academic stress among Health Science Preparatory Program students in two Saudi universities.
